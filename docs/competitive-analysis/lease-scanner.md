## scanitfree Lease Red Flag Scanner — Summary

The scanitfree Lease Red Flag Scanner is a **web-based, paste-text, no-signup AI tool** that accepts pasted lease text and returns an A–F fairness grade (0–100 score), clause-by-clause red flags with three severity tiers (danger / warning / info), each with the exact clause quoted, a plain-English explanation, and a 💡 suggestion. It also surfaces a `missingProtections` list for standard tenant protections absent from the document, and a 2–3 sentence summary. Powered by Claude Sonnet 4. Free at 15 scans/day per IP. No account required.

**Prompt focus:** Excessive fees, unfair termination, waived tenant rights, maintenance responsibility shifts, unreasonable entry, security deposit issues, hidden auto-renewal terms, and missing standard protections. **No jurisdiction awareness. No document type distinction. No counter-proposal language generation.**

## What scanitfree Has That Competitors Don't

1. **A–F fairness grade AND a numeric score together** — Only LeaseGuard AI also uses A–F; every other competitor uses numeric score, risk label, or no score at all. The letter grade makes the assessment viscerally legible — a tenant receiving an "F" lease knows immediately what they're dealing with. No other free competitor pairs the letter with a numeric for dual readability.

2. **`suggestion` field per flag — a prescription in the free tier** — This is scanitfree's most underappreciated output field. The `suggestion` (rendered with 💡) is a natural-language negotiation hint included on every flag, for free. SaferLease and LeaseGuard AI charge $19–$19.99 to unlock equivalent guidance. ClearLease and Justee.ai also include it free, but scanitfree is one of very few zero-friction tools that pairs "here's the problem" with "here's what to say" without a paywall — though the language is less specific than true word-for-word scripts.

3. **`missingProtections` array — surfaces what's absent, not just what's wrong** — No competitor explicitly shows a list of standard tenant protections the lease *failed to include*. Justee.ai partially does this via its 180+ compliance points. But surfacing missing protections as a distinct named list ("No rent increase notice required," "No habitability warranty stated") is a unique output that shifts the tool from reactive (flagging bad clauses) to proactive (what a good lease should have included). This is a genuine differentiator.

4. **15 free scans/day per IP with no account, no credit card, no email** — SaferLease limits free output to top 3 flags; Flag Red caps at 1/month; ClearLease at 3/month; goHeather requires account creation. scanitfree offers the highest free-tier throughput in the niche with the least friction.

5. **Powered by Claude Sonnet 4 — longer effective context window** — Lease agreements regularly run 5,000–15,000+ words. The 8,000 character input limit cuts many full leases off (a critical UX problem — see gaps below), but when the full text fits, Claude's context handling and instruction-following produces cleaner structured JSON than GPT-4-based competitors. Marketable to privacy-conscious users as "not OpenAI."

## Does "Diagnosis Free, Prescription Paid" Hold in the Legal Niche?

**Yes — but with a lease-specific twist that makes the pattern more pronounced and more monetizable than in resume tools.**

In the resume niche, the prescription is "rewrite this bullet." In the lease niche, the prescription is "here is the exact sentence to say to your landlord" — and the emotional/financial stakes (multi-year binding contract, thousands of dollars in deposits, housing security) make users far more willing to pay for it.

**The pattern as it manifests across lease tools:**

| Tool | Free = Diagnosis | Paid = Prescription | Gate Mechanism |
|---|---|---|---|
| SaferLease | Risk score + top 3 flags | Negotiation tips per clause + PDF | $19 one-time |
| LeaseGuard AI | A–F score + top flags + state rights | Word-for-word scripts + citations + full PDF | $19.99 one-time |
| ClearLease | 3 scans/month + scripts included | Unlimited scans + PDF download | $4.99/month |
| goHeather | Basic risk flagging | Deeper analysis with token limits | $29.99/month |
| Flag Red | 1 scan + guidance | Saved results + reports | $3/contract |
| Justee.ai | Single-state compliance review | Multistate analysis | $19/month |

**Key structural insight for scanitfree:** The most successful lease tools don't just gate on "see more flags" — they gate on **negotiation leverage**. The premium deliverable is not *more analysis* but *what to do about it*: the words to write in an email to your landlord, the clause rewrite to propose, the state statute to cite. This is a psychologically distinct product (action vs. information) that justifies payment even when users already know what's wrong.

**Current scanitfree position:** The `suggestion` field partially delivers prescription in the free tier, but the suggestions are general advice ("negotiate a cap on this fee") rather than specific scripts ("Request that the landlord replace Section 8.2 with: 'Late fees shall not exceed $50 or 5% of monthly rent, whichever is less, per California Civil Code § 1671'"). That specificity gap is the paid-tier opportunity.

## Recommended Additions

Prioritized by impact-to-effort, competitive gap urgency, and paid-tier monetization potential:

---

### 🥇 #1 — PDF Upload (via Claude Native Document API)
**Rationale — critical credibility gap:** Leases are universally distributed as PDFs. No one copies a 40-page lease into a textarea. The 8,000 character input limit compounds the problem: many full leases exceed this limit, meaning scanitfree is silently analyzing *partial* leases without telling the user. This undermines trust in the results. Every single competitor accepts PDF. Claude's Messages API natively supports PDF documents as `document` content blocks — this is a one-route change with no third-party dependency.

**Also fix:** Raise or remove the 8,000 character hard truncation for the lease tool specifically. A full residential lease averages 5,000–12,000 words (~7,000–17,000 characters). Silent truncation means scanitfree is reviewing half a lease and giving it an A. Add a visible character counter and a "your lease was truncated" warning at minimum, or switch to file upload + no truncation limit.

**Implementation path:** Add file input to `client.tsx`. In `route.ts`, detect file attachment and send it as a `{"type": "document", "source": {"type": "base64", "media_type": "application/pdf", "data": "..."}}` content block. Existing text-paste path unchanged. Claude reads the PDF natively — no external OCR service needed.

---

### 🥈 #2 — State/Jurisdiction Selector + Tenant Law Grounding
**Rationale — biggest free-tier credibility gap:** The single most damaging trust gap for the legal niche. Tenant law varies dramatically by state: California caps security deposits at 2× rent; Texas has no cap. New York City mandates 24-hour entry notice; many states only require "reasonable" notice. Without knowing the user's state, scanitfree's flags may be wrong (flagging something legal in the user's state) or miss the most important violations (state-specific illegal clauses not flagged). Competitors who do this — LeaseGuard AI (50 states), TurboTenant (state selector), Justee.ai (180+ law points) — are significantly more credible on the "is this legal" question.

**Implementation path:** Add a `<select>` for U.S. state (or "Other / International") above the textarea. Pass `state` in the API request body. In the prompt, inject: "The lease is governed by [STATE] law. Reference specific [STATE] landlord-tenant statutes where relevant (e.g., [STATE] security deposit limits, required notice periods, habitability standards). Flag any clauses that violate [STATE] law specifically." This requires no external data fetching — Claude's training includes state landlord-tenant law knowledge. For the paid tier, add statutory citations as a gated field.

---

### 🥉 #3 — Word-for-Word Negotiation Scripts (Paid Tier Gate)
**Rationale — highest-conversion paid feature in the niche:** SaferLease ($19) and LeaseGuard AI ($19.99) both sell this and both have active user bases. The emotional driver: a tenant with 3 red flags on their lease doesn't just want to know there's a problem — they're anxious about confronting their landlord and want the exact words to say. "Request that the landlord replace Section 12.4 with: 'Landlord shall provide 24 hours written notice before entering the premises, except in emergency situations as defined by [STATE] Civil Code § XXXX.'" is worth paying for. The current scanitfree `suggestion` field hints at this but falls short of actionable specificity.

**Implementation path:** Add `"script": "<word-for-word negotiation script>|null"` to each flag's JSON schema. Expand the prompt: "For each 'danger' flag, generate a `script` field containing a word-for-word email sentence or clause replacement the tenant can use to request a change." Gate the display of `script` fields behind a paid account. Free users see: the flag, the explanation, the 💡 suggestion, and a "🔒 Unlock negotiation script" CTA button. Paid users see the full script inline. Price: $9.99 one-time per lease (lower than SaferLease/LeaseGuard) or $7.99/month for power users.

---

### 🏅 #4 — Document Type + Audience Selector (Residential / Commercial / Sublease)
**Rationale — precision and SEO signal:** SaferLease is the only direct competitor that explicitly handles 6 document types. The prompt for a commercial lease review is fundamentally different from a residential one: commercial leases have no implied warranty of habitability, CAM charge scrutiny matters, personal guarantee language is critical, and the power imbalance reverses (sophisticated parties expected). A sublease has different missing-protections expectations. Adding a simple 3-option selector ("Residential lease / Commercial lease / Sublease") allows the prompt to be tailored without any infrastructure change — the JSON output schema stays identical.

**Implementation path:** Add a 3-button type selector above the textarea. Pass `leaseType` in the API body. In `route.ts`, maintain three lean prompt variants for the lease-scanner tool — or inject the type into the existing prompt: "This is a [TYPE] lease. Evaluate accordingly: [type-specific instruction]." The residential variant stays as-is. The commercial variant removes habitability references and adds: "Check for CAM charge caps, personal guarantee scope, co-tenancy clauses, and exclusivity provisions." The sublease variant checks: sublandlord liability exposure, master lease pass-through terms, subletting consent clauses.

---

### 🎖️ #5 — Explicit Privacy / Auto-Delete Trust Signal
**Rationale — conversion blocker, cheap to fix:** Leases contain personally identifiable information — tenant name, address, SSN in some cases, financial details. SaferLease, ClearLease, Justee.ai, LeaseGuard AI, and Flag Red all prominently display auto-delete guarantees (24h–post-analysis) and encryption specifics (AES-256). scanitfree's current UI has no privacy messaging about what happens to pasted lease text. This is a conversion blocker for privacy-conscious users who hesitate before pasting a sensitive legal document. The fix is a single line of UI copy — no backend change required since the tool already doesn't persist input.

**Implementation path:** Add a small lock icon + one-line disclaimer below the textarea: "🔒 Your lease text is never stored — analyzed in memory only and discarded immediately after." Optionally add it to the page metadata description for SEO ("private, no-storage lease review"). This costs 10 minutes of engineering time and likely increases submit conversion rate meaningfully for this specific tool, where trust friction is higher than for food or resume tools.
